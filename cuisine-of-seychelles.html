Traditional cuisine of the Seychelles

  

Most visitors to the Seychelles islands fell in love with the food. From fresh fish, seafood, meat, fruits and vegetables, there is definetely something for every taste bud and palate. it is therefore extremely important that you taste those delicious foods as much as you can.

  

Whether you are residing in a hotel, guest house, or self catering accomodation, you have equal chance to enjoy those delicatesse. If you are residing in a hotel or guest house, those food are already on the menu. If you are residing in a self catering accomodation you can do so by having lunch or dinner at some restaurants who prepares traditional food, especially for visitors.

  

Here is a compilation of some of the best traditional cuisine of the Seychelles. don't forget to add them on your menu while your are on holidays under the sun.

    Fresh grilled fish: Prepared over the hot coals of a BBQ, and marinated with fresh spices and chillies. Salted fish: Cooked with a mixture of spices and can be accomodated to a number of dishes. Coconut Curry: Can be cocoked with fish or meat, based around masala, spices, curry eaves, and coconut milk. Lentil: The red lentils is a popular staple, an act as a server side with many dishes. Sausage Rougay: is a mixture of tomato, onion sauce, garlic, ginger and chilli cooked with sausages. Bouyon Brede: A soup made from a variery of spinaches Rich in spices. Shark chatini: Boiled shark meat, cocoked with lime juice, fried onions, bilimbi, spices, and chillies. Kat-kat banana: It is usually prepared with plantains, which are first boiled with fresh coconut milk, then mashed and combined with cooked, shredded fish fillets. Salade de palmiste: Made from hearts of palm trees. A delicious salad which includes green mangoes, lime juice, green tomatoes and spices. Octopus curry: Cooked in coconut milk and spices and served with rice. Bat curry: Prepared with chunks of skinned fruit bat that are marinated in vinegar and red wine, and then cooked with a variety of herbs and spices. Ladob: A sweet dessert made from bananas, breadfruits, and cassave. Cooked in coconut milk.
